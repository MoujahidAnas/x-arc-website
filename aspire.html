Aspire People had a pipeline problem

Aspire People places education staff across the UK. Teaching assistants, supply teachers, SEN specialists. Every candidate that walks through the door needs the same thing: screening, compliance checks, interview coordination, and documentation. The work is critical and it never stops.

Each candidate requires 65–120 minutes of manual work. CV review, initial screening, DBS verification, safeguarding checks, right to work confirmation, reference collection, interview scheduling, and candidate profile generation. At thousands of candidates, that's a full team doing the same work on repeat.

Education recruitment has the heaviest compliance burden in UK staffing. DBS Enhanced checks, Child Workforce clearances, safeguarding training verification, regulated activity references. You can't skip any of it. You can't shortcut any of it. Every candidate, every time.

Each candidate takes 65–120 minutes from sourcing to placement-ready. At thousands of candidates, that's a full team doing the same work on repeat.

They tried the obvious solutions. AI tools that could screen CVs. Chatbots that could answer candidate questions. Scheduling tools that could book interviews. Each one solved a piece. Nobody gave them the whole pipeline.

The full pipeline. Eight stages. Zero gaps.

CCAP doesn't automate one step. It runs the entire recruitment pipeline end-to-end. Every candidate goes through all eight stages automatically. Nothing falls through the cracks.

01

Sourcing

Monitors job boards around the clock. Downloads new CVs as they appear. Finds candidates before the team wakes up.

02

Processing

Parses every CV, extracts structured data. Name, qualifications, location, contact. Detects duplicates so no candidate gets contacted twice.

03

Evaluation

Reads each CV against the open job description. Scores candidates 0–100 on fit. Filters out unsuitable candidates with documented reasons.

04

Outreach

Sends a personalised WhatsApp message to every qualified candidate. Follows up automatically after 23 hours if no response. No candidate falls through the cracks.

05

Screening

Sarah — the candidate-facing agent CCAP deployed — conducts the full prequalification interview via WhatsApp. Covers role preferences, availability, qualifications, DBS, references, right to work. Handles voice notes and document photos.

Candidates don't know they're talking to AI. Most don't care. They get a fast, professional experience and move through the pipeline in minutes instead of days. 85% of engaged candidates complete the full screening.

06

Interviews

Books Microsoft Teams interviews with the assigned consultant. Sends calendar invites and reminders. Monitors attendance — catches no-shows and offers to reschedule.

07

Documentation

After the interview, generates a professional, client-ready candidate profile from the transcript and screening data. Sends it for quality review. No consultant writes a word.

08

CRM sync

Every status update, document, and communication syncs to the CRM in real-time. Consultants see the full picture in their existing system. No switching tools.

3,000 candidates in. The pipeline is still getting faster.

CCAP has processed over 3,000 candidates. Every one of them made the pipeline better.

Outreach messages that got responses were reinforced. Screening questions that felt awkward were reworded. Follow-up timing was adjusted based on when candidates actually reply. The compliance flow was tightened so it feels like a natural conversation, not an interrogation. None of this was manually tuned. CCAP studied what worked and adjusted.

The result: early screenings averaged 15–18 minutes. Current screenings average 12. Candidate completion rate climbed from 70% to 85%. Outreach response rates improved as CCAP learned which messages land and which get ignored.
